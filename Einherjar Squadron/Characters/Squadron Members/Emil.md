## Bio
“Emil” was born on [[Midgard]], the most populated planet in the Yggdrasil system. Born to simple agricultural workers, who lived modest but not meager lives. Nothing remarkable could be found about the first 6 years of life, not even the subject’s birth name; it is possible that any information on this period was purged due to what events would follow. Merely a few days after the subject’s 6th birthday it was taken by unknown persons, later discovered to be agents of the oligarchs of the planet of [[Asgard]]. Due to the lack of information of the parents after this point, it is theorized that the subject’s parents were disposed of as well. The subject then goes effectively missing for the following 10 years, only to reemerge as the weapon known as Fenrir. What exactly happened to the subject during this blank period is unknown, but certain inferences can be made due to other information gathered. We believe the subject received cybernetic enhancement, gene therapy, and mental programming at the hands of Smith-Shimano Corpro. Given the discovery of a contract between SSC and the oligarchs of [[Asgard]], it is our belief that the Aesir, as they were referred to, desired the creation of super soldiers, to be used as tools to control the masses. With the emergence of these new human weapons, the already tense relationship between these Aesir and the lower class boiled over. A rebellion began to form, referred to the denizens of Yggdrasil as Ragnarök, an undertaking which the Aesir thought foolish, believing themselves invincible with the indoctrinated super soldiers at their beck and call. This arrogance would be their downfall. A defector scientist with knowledge of the mental programming undergone by these human weapons, was able to slowly turn them into assets for the rebellion. With their greatest tools stolen, the tide of war quickly turned against the Aesir. Little data could be gathered about the events of the closing months and in particular the final battle of the war, but we are quite certain the subject had some hand in them. We may never discover what truly occurred, but what we are certain of is that it resulted with a massive portion of the planet being blown off and the rest being rendered mostly uninhabitable. Records also show that the final broadcast sent from the planet was simply one word “Fenrir” followed by a blood curdling scream. While the subject becomes unaccounted for in the immediate aftermath of the war, we will note the effects on the system a whole. While most of the planetary bodies suffered some devastation, they would see relative prosperity that would last to the present time. Additionally tales of the monstrous “Fenrir” began to spread in the system, creating something of a bogeyman for small children to be frightened of. The name Fenrir reappears 8 months later in a far away star system, now referring to a mercenary, one quickly gaining a reputation for being exceedingly proficient.
