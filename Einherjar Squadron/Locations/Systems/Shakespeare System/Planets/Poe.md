### Inhabited, Knight world, part of [[Shakespeare System]]

## Courts, Knights, and chivalry

Courts are collections of Knights serving underneath a singular **Lord**. Courts govern land, field armies, and handle production within their territory. So to live in Poe is to live under a court. **Knights** are the top dogs of a court, the best of the best. Knights can be pilots, artisans, politicians, intellectuals, ect.

Honor is of utmost importance to the upper class of Poe, resolving disputes through duels of all kinds, the most respected of which are Lancer duels. All Lords (and most knights) are pilots owning their own top of the line mechs. Official duels cost more than honor, often times property and titles are directly tied to a knight’s performance in the ring.

## Indentured servants

Part of the honor based government on Poe is making sure that any and all debt is payed, and with interest of enforcing that value **The Round Table** (the governing collection of Courts that make planet wide policies) enacted the **debtor labor program** or DLP. This program allows Courts, Lords, Knights, or organizations to [purchase labor from debtors](https://media1.tenor.com/m/LXXI3mL2hygAAAAd/rick-and-morty-that-just-sounds-like-slavery-with-extra-steps.gif).

Indentured servants do have rights, and there are measures to make sure that they are not just slaves.

1. There must be a defined job, with defined duties, before indenture ship can start
2. Indentured must be housed, fed, reasonably comfortable, and given medical assistance if needed
3. As soon as their debt is payed, they are a full and free citizen of POE
4. No direct physical or mental abuse may be directed at indentured (this does not apply to jobs with direct risks such as military service)

The form of crime and punishment on Poe is based on this system, commit a crime and a bounty is put on your head. Anyone may “purchase” you for your bounty, you then owe that entity the amount of the bounty and become indentured to them until debt free. This is enforced using a high tech collar with a neutralizing device embedded within. Triggering this will cause a full scale investigation into both the indentured and the employer, so this rarely happens.

## Tournaments and duels

All official titles, within and outside of courts, are decided through tests of prowess in a relevant field. For artisans it may be a display of their craft, for a scholar a test of their intellect, however for just about anyone else its a duel via mech. From production ownership to government positions a mech contest of some sort is the deciding factor of your worthiness. Most of the time, your opponent will be whoever has the position to begin with.

Poe also has Tournaments between the courts to decide both Round Table positions and general pecking order. Every match between courts comes with an ante. This could be anything from land to servants. These tournaments often time replace war between courts in their entirety.

## War

War within Poe happens exclusively between Courts with enough animosity that a duel or tournament can’t settle the disagreement. War however, is considered “civil” by other societies standards. Both sides must declare the conflict with The Round Table, as well as declaring intended outcomes and forfeits (land, titles, ect). Once negotiated the two courts will enter war until there is an official forfeit, or one of the two lords is killed.

All out war is HIGHLY looked down upon by the society of Poe, often times being seen as a barbaric alternative to duels. However this attitude is exclusive to conflicts within the society, there has been times where the Round Table has declared war on external threats.
